1 Commodities Portfolio Intelligence

Integrating Time Series Forecasting, Multi-Objective Decision Making & Reinforcement Learning

An innovative research initiative at FAE Business School developing patent-ready methodologies for agricultural commodities portfolio optimization through advanced econometric and machine learning techniques.

Explore Our Research → View Publications →

1.1 🎯 Research Vision

This Scientific Initiation Project (PAIC - Programa de Apoio à Iniciação Científica) represents the continuation and evolution of pioneering work initiated at PUCPR, now advancing at FAE Business School with enhanced scope and objectives.

1.1.1 🎓 Academic Goals

Primary Objectives:

  • Patent Development: Create innovative methodologies for multi-period portfolio optimization
  • Scientific Publications: Contribute to academic literature on agricultural finance
  • Doctoral Research Integration: Support ongoing PhD thesis development
  • Undergraduate Excellence: Train students in cutting-edge quantitative finance

Institutional Context:

This project builds upon the foundation established at PUCPR (visit the previous project site) while expanding into new frontiers of agricultural commodity portfolio management.

1.2 🔬 Core Research Framework

Our methodology integrates three fundamental pillars:

1.2.1 📈 Time Series Forecasting

Advanced predictive modeling for commodity prices using:

  • ARIMA/GARCH family models
  • Bayesian structural time series
  • LSTM and GRU neural networks
  • Ensemble forecasting methods
  • Hawkes processes for news impact

Econometrics Deep Learning Bayesian Methods

1.2.2 🎯 Multi-Objective Optimization

Portfolio optimization considering multiple conflicting objectives:

  • Return maximization
  • Risk minimization
  • Liquidity management
  • Sustainability criteria
  • Dynamic rebalancing strategies

Evolutionary Algorithms Pareto Optimization Fuzzy Logic

1.2.3 🤖 Reinforcement Learning

Adaptive decision-making strategies:

  • Q-Learning for portfolio selection
  • Deep Q-Networks (DQN)
  • Policy gradient methods
  • Multi-agent systems
  • Dynamic strategy adaptation

RL Algorithms Adaptive Systems Decision Theory

## 📊 Key Commodities Focus

Our research concentrates on Brazilian agricultural grain commodities:

```{r commodity-focus}
#| echo: false
#| warning: false
#| message: false
#| fig-height: 4

library(tidyverse)
library(plotly)

# Commodities data
commodities <- data.frame(
  Commodity = c("Soybeans", "Corn", "Wheat", "Coffee", "Sugar"),
  Market_Importance = c(95, 88, 75, 82, 85),
  Volatility = c(72, 78, 65, 88, 80),
  Liquidity = c(90, 85, 70, 75, 82)
) %>%
  pivot_longer(cols = -Commodity, names_to = "Metric", values_to = "Score")

p <- ggplot(commodities, aes(x = Commodity, y = Score, fill = Metric)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity", position = "dodge") +
  theme_minimal() +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c("#003d7a", "#4a90e2", "#ff6b35")) +
  labs(title = "Key Commodities Analysis",
       subtitle = "Market Importance, Volatility & Liquidity Scores",
       y = "Index Score (0-100)",
       x = "") +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(hjust = 0.5, face = "bold", size = 14),
    plot.subtitle = element_text(hjust = 0.5, size = 11),
    legend.position = "top"
  ) +
  coord_flip()

ggplotly(p) %>%
  layout(legend = list(orientation = "h", x = 0.3, y = 1.1))

```
